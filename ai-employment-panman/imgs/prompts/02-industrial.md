A large textile factory interior from the 1830s-1850s. Rows of power looms and spinning machines fill the floor. Workers (men, women, children) tend to the machines — feeding thread, monitoring tension, collecting output. High ceilings with iron columns, large windows letting in light, steam rising from machinery. The scale is overwhelming — dozens of machines, hundreds of workers reduced to small figures by the industrial space around them.

The mood: awe-inspiring industrial power, but also the beginning of the end for artisan cottage workers. This is the moment when machines first replaced skilled human hands.

Style: Fine-line engraving aesthetic, cross-hatching for shadows, high contrast black and white. Editorial historical illustration feel. Not photographic — illustrated like a 19th century newspaper woodcut.

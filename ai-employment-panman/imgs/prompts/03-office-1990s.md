Vintage photograph style, desaturated/sepia-toned. A 1990s office environment.

An open-plan office floor with rows of identical cubicles. Each cubicle has a large boxy CRT monitor, keyboard, mouse pad, and stacks of printed papers. Workers in 1990s business casual clothing (shoulder pads, polo shirts, ties) sit at their stations, typing or talking on desk phones with coiled cords. Fluorescent overhead lighting. Beige plastic everywhere — the computers, the cubicle walls, the phones.

The details that feel dated: floppy disk holders on desks, printed spreadsheets pinned to cubicle walls, a dot matrix printer in the background, coffee mugs with corporate logos.

Mood: the height of the information age — this is what "modern work" looked like before smartphones. Slightly nostalgic, slightly absurd in retrospect.

Style: Documentary photography aesthetic. Slightly grainy, slightly warm-toned, natural office lighting with fluorescent green cast. Shot as if by a photojournalist documenting office culture. Realistic and observational.
